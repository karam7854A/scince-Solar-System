Mars – The Red Planet

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is known for its reddish appearance due to iron oxide on its surface.

It has the largest volcano in the Solar System, Olympus Mons, which stands 22 km (13.6 miles) high.

Mars has a thin atmosphere composed mainly of carbon dioxide, which makes it unable to retain heat.

Its average surface temperature is about -63°C (-81°F), with extreme variations.

Mars has massive dust storms that can cover the entire planet and last for months.

Scientists believe Mars once had liquid water, and evidence of ancient riverbeds has been found.

It has two small, irregularly shaped moons, Phobos and Deimos.

Mars is a key focus for space exploration, with rovers and orbiters searching for signs of past life.
